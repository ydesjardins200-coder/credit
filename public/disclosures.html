What iBoost is

iBoost is a credit-building subscription service. When you make your monthly payment on time, we report that on-time payment to consumer credit reporting agencies (Equifax, TransUnion, and Experian in the United States; Equifax and TransUnion in Canada). On-time payment history is a major component of your credit score, and consistent on-time reporting over time is designed to help you build or rebuild your credit profile.

What iBoost is not

iBoost is not a lender and does not extend credit, issue loans, or provide a line of credit. iBoost is not a credit repair organization as defined under the U.S. Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) or similar provincial laws in Canada; we do not promise to remove accurate information from your credit file. iBoost is not a registered financial advisor, investment dealer, tax advisor, or legal advisor, and we do not provide individualized financial, tax, or legal advice. Any education content on the site is for general informational purposes only.

Credit score results will vary

Credit score outcomes depend on many factors outside iBoost's control, including your full credit history, existing debts, other reported accounts, and future behaviour. While consistent on-time payments are widely recognized as improving credit scores over time, iBoost cannot guarantee any specific score increase, approval for future credit products, or favourable interest rates. Late payments made to iBoost will be reported to the bureaus and may negatively affect your score.

Fees and billing

iBoost is billed monthly. Current pricing, billing terms, refund policy, and cancellation rights are described in our Terms of Service. You may cancel at any time; cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing cycle.

Data and privacy

We collect and process personal information (including identity and financial data) to provide the service and to report to credit bureaus. Full details are in our Privacy Policy. We do not sell your personal information. Your data is protected in transit by bank-level (256-bit) TLS encryption and at rest by our infrastructure providers' encryption-at-rest standards.

Canadian consumers

iBoost services available in Canada are offered in accordance with applicable federal and provincial consumer-protection laws, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and Quebec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector (Law 25). Credit reporting in Canada is governed by provincial credit reporting acts.

U.S. consumers

iBoost services available in the United States are offered in accordance with applicable federal and state laws, including the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and the Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA). Under the FCRA, you have the right to dispute inaccurate information on your credit report directly with the credit reporting agencies at no cost.
