Why iBoost

Credit building is simpler once you know what actually moves your score.

The four things below are where most people get stuck — and what we built iBoost to fix.

Problem → fix

On-time payments reported to every major bureau.

Your score is determined by what lenders see on your report. Many credit-builders only report to one bureau — so two-thirds of lenders never see your progress. We report to all three U.S. bureaus (Equifax, TransUnion, Experian) and both Canadian bureaus (Equifax and TransUnion) at the same point in every monthly reporting cycle.

Receipt: 35% of your FICO score and about 40% of your VantageScore come from payment history. A payment that isn't reported is a payment that doesn't help your score. myFICO source↗
Credit math

A free budget tool that pairs with your credit journey.

Credit utilization — the percentage of your available credit you're using — drives about 30% of your score. You can't get utilization right if you don't track income against expenses in the first place. Most credit-builders ignore this. We include a full-featured budget app free, forever, because knowing what you can afford is a prerequisite to building credit.

Receipt: Utilization under 30% is the generally-accepted threshold; under 10% is where scores start climbing aggressively. Achieving either without tracking spending is a coin flip. myFICO source↗
Personalized guidance

Month-by-month advice based on your actual profile.

Credit building is sequential, not parallel. The advice that matters in month 1 (set up autopay, avoid new credit) differs from what helps in month 8 (request a limit increase, optimize utilization timing). Most apps show the same three tips forever. Ours references your bureau data, payment history, current utilization, and account tenure — changing recommendations as your profile evolves.

Receipt: The five factors in a FICO score have different relative weights at different score ranges. A 580 score improves from different actions than a 680 score. We know the difference. myFICO source↗
Transparent education

Plain-language explanations of what bureaus actually score.

Credit scoring is obscured by jargon — "hard pulls," "tradelines," "derogatory marks," "utilization ratio." Most of it describes simple ideas in complicated ways, and most of it is taught only by people trying to sell you something. Our library explains every scoring factor and every bureau action in plain language, cites the original source when relevant, and stays free forever. No email gate, no course purchase, no upsell path.

Receipt: The specific formulas behind FICO and VantageScore are public information published by the scoring agencies themselves. We cite them directly, not through marketing paraphrase. myFICO source↗

Laws we operate under

Canadian privacy law Active

Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act and the provincial privacy statutes that run alongside it

We handle all Canadian user data in accordance with PIPEDA's ten fair information principles — consent, limited collection, accuracy, safeguards, individual access, and openness. Users may request access to or correction of their personal information at any time by emailing privacy@iboost.com. Full practices documented in our Privacy Policy.

We also meet the stronger obligations that apply in specific provinces, notably Quebec's Law 25 and the provincial PIPAs in Alberta and British Columbia. This means: the right to be informed of any automated decision-making that affects you and to request a human review; data portability in a structured, commonly used format; and breach notification within 72 hours of confirming scope.

Federal regulator: Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada↗ · Provincial (Quebec): Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec↗

FCRA Active

Fair Credit Reporting Act — 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.

We report credit data to the three U.S. nationwide credit reporting agencies (Equifax, TransUnion, Experian) accurately and on the reporting schedule defined for each furnisher. Under FCRA § 611, users have the right to dispute any information we report. Disputes submitted to us directly are investigated within 30 days at no charge. Full process in our Disclosures.

Regulators: CFPB↗ · FTC↗

U.S. legal framework Active

Federal and state frameworks that apply to our service in the United States

iBoost is a credit-building product, not a Credit Repair Organization as defined under CROA (15 U.S.C. § 1679 et seq.). We help users establish positive credit history through reported on-time payments. We do not claim to remove accurate negative information from anyone's credit report, we do not dispute items on behalf of users for a fee, and we do not charge for any service before it is rendered.

Beyond that federal clarification, we operate across all fifty U.S. states and comply with applicable state-level consumer-protection, financial-services, and privacy statutes where they apply to our service category. Notable frameworks we observe include the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and CPRA, the Colorado Privacy Act, the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act, and equivalent statutes in Connecticut, Utah, and other states. State-specific rights (access, deletion, opt-out of sale) are fulfilled through the process described in our Privacy Policy.

CROA statute: 15 U.S.C. § 1679↗

Encryption & infrastructure

TLS 1.3 — encryption in transit Active

All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted end-to-end using TLS 1.3, the current IETF standard. TLS 1.3 eliminates deprecated cipher suites and reduces the handshake to a single round-trip.

Provider: Netlify / Cloudflare edge · Standard: IETF RFC 8446↗

AES-256 — encryption at rest Active

Your personal information is stored encrypted at the database layer using AES-256. This is the encryption standard approved by the U.S. NSA for classified information up to Top Secret, and required by HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and most federal compliance frameworks.
