What's actually happening

Four mechanics do the heavy lifting.

Those 92 points don't come from magic. They come from four credit-scoring levers that work together, month after month. Here's what each one is doing while you play the animation.

35% of your score

On-time payment history

Every month you pay iBoost on time, another line is added to your payment-history record. By month 6 you have a run of 6 on-time payments across all three bureaus — that's when lenders start treating you differently.

Source: myFICO factor weights
30% of your score

Utilization ratio

Your reported credit line gives you headroom you didn't have before. With iBoost Essential's $750 or Complete's $2,000 added to your total available credit, the balance you carry on existing cards drops as a percentage — and that drop is measured in real points on the next bureau refresh.

Tip in animation: Month 3 — "Keep your utility card balance under 30% of the limit."
15% of your score

Length of credit history

Every month the iBoost tradeline ages on your file, and the average-age-of-accounts metric ticks up. This is why we tell members in month 5 not to close their oldest card — credit age compounds and the oldest account matters most.

Tip in animation: Month 5 — "Don't close your oldest card — credit age matters more than you think."
10% each — new credit & mix

Inquiry discipline & credit diversity

The tips in months 9 and 10 exist because hard inquiries and the wrong kind of new credit can stall your climb in the final stretch. iBoost's AI flags these decisions before you make them — this is where a coach matters more than a calculator.

Tip in animation: Month 9 — "Almost there. Resist opening new credit in the last 3 months."
The first month

What happens in the first thirty days.

Sign-up to your first reported on-time payment. Day-by-day.

  1. Day 0 You sign up (no credit check).

    Identity is verified via a soft pull that doesn't generate a hard inquiry on your report. Account created, plan selected.

  2. Day 1 – 3 Your iBoost tradeline is opened.

    Your reported credit line ($750 Essential or $2,000 Complete) is attached to your profile. Bureaus are notified.

  3. Day 14 Your first payment posts.

    Autopay collects the monthly amount. Payment is logged internally and queued for the month-end bureau report.

  4. Day 30 On-time payment reported to all bureaus.

    Equifax, TransUnion, and (in the US) Experian receive the report. Your credit file now shows iBoost as an active account in good standing.

  5. Day 30 – 45 First score movement visible.

    Bureaus refresh their scoring calculations on their own cycle — usually within 2–3 weeks of your report. You'll see the change in your iBoost dashboard first.
